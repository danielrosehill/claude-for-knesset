## What each area argues

**[Rental market & tenancy law](./rental-market/index.md)** — the tenant's deteriorating
position is driven less by monthly rent than by the cost and frequency of the *transaction*,
and the transaction recurs every time a landlord declines to renew. Better evidenced and
harder to rebut than "rents are too high", and it points at cheaper instruments than rent
control.

**[Public transport & the commute](./public-transport/index.md)** — transport is argued in
Israel as a capital-spending question, but the marginal decision to leave the car at home is
made on cost, legibility, comfort and dignity. Those are set by enforcement policy, fare
design and information design, not by track kilometres.

**[Political accountability](./political-accountability/index.md)** — the structural
precondition for everything else. A closed-list system with one nationwide district gives a
voter no lever against any individual legislator, which is what lets cost of living and
service delivery be crowded out by coalition management.

**[Citizens first](./citizens-first/index.md)** — civil preparedness that failed under fire,
set against an apparatus whose declared beneficiary is world Jewry rather than the public
that funds it. The deliverable is a reallocation proposal with a costed preparedness
programme attached, not a critique of diaspora policy in the abstract.

**[Open government data](./open-government-data/index.md)** — Israel has a
freedom-of-information law and a national data portal. The problem is that what gets
published is decoupled from what anyone would use: the infrastructure of transparency,
stocked with datasets that hold no one to account.

**[Environmental quality](./environmental-quality/index.md)** — the distinctive plank is not
that noise is bad, but that a resident living through months of excavation cannot discover
what was permitted, what mitigation was conditioned on it, or whether anyone checks. Process
failures have cheap fixes.

## How the areas connect

They are not six independent complaints. Open government data and environmental quality share
a mechanism — data you cannot get is a lever you cannot pull — and are deliberately
cross-referenced rather than merged. Environmental quality and public transport share the
modal-shift case. Political accountability sits underneath all of them.

The [comparison matrices](../context/matrices/index.md) are where each proposal gets checked
against what every other party is offering, on the same scale, including where someone else
has the better instrument.
